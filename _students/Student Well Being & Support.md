The objectives of student well-being and support are to provide care across three tiers:

**Tier 1: School-wide Well-being Support**

The Filiae Centre promotes student well-being through school-wide initiatives, and educational and career guidance programmes that help students build self-awareness, resilience, and lifelong wellness practices. One such programme is the CareShare Series, which is run across all year levels and facilitated by the Peer Helpers. Through classroom-based sessions, students develop skills to better understand themselves, relate to others, and build healthy intra- and interpersonal relationships..

**Tier 2: Focused Group Support through Thrive Sessions**

The Filiae Centre also empowers students to grow holistically through Thrive Sessions, which include focused small-group workshops, programmes, and activities. These include:

*   ChillArt Rainforest: A learning experience using different forms of art to support self-compassion and self-awareness
    
*   Emotional Regulation Skills Support Group
    
*   Hangout with the Peer Helpers: Small group activities such as study sessions, movie screening and discussions led by our Peer Helpers and supervised by the Filiae Centre Staff
    
*   Slumber Par-tea: A sleep education workshop aimed at fostering better sleep quality and healthy rest habits
    

**Tier 3: Individual Counselling Support**

The counsellors from the Filiae Centre provide individual counselling and personalised support for students who may need assistance with social-emotional development and/or learning differences.

Through these tiers, the Filiae Centre aims to nurture a caring school community by offering life-coaching opportunities and equipping students, teachers, and parents with skills to support one another.

# Peer Helpers

![](/images/phelp.png)

Trained by our school counselors, peer helpers play a crucial role in promoting a caring school environment and fostering relationships among students. These girls provide support, guidance, and assistance to their fellow students, serving as a valuable resource for peers dealing with various issues. In doing so, they contribute significantly to creating a supportive and inclusive atmosphere within the school.
