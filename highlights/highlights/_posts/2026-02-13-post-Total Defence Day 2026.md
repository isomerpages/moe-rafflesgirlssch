<p>To mark Total Defence Day, RGS organised a simulated disruption exercise
where students experienced a school day without electricity or digital
connectivity. The activity offered a glimpse into the challenges people
may face during times of crisis, encouraging students to reflect on resilience
and adaptability.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/22.png">
</div>
<p>Lessons continued across the campus using natural light and outdoor spaces,
where students gathered in groups to work through themed quizzes and worksheets
about the significance of Total Defence. Through these interactive activities,
students explored Singapore’s history while learning about the values behind
this important national observance.</p>
<p>Students from Food and Design brought the experience to life by preparing
dishes over charcoal fires outdoors. Huddling over their personal cooking
pans, students experimented with their own menu of Cheesy Gim satay, army
stew and even toasted marshmallow treats.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Year 1 students also took part in a series of Total Defence station games
led by the school’s Uniformed Group CCAs. These activities challenged participants
to work together and think on their feet, from navigating obstacles while
blindfolded to testing their precision in cup-shooting challenges.</p>

<p>By stepping outside their usual routines, students developed a deeper
appreciation for preparedness, teamwork and resilience—values that lie
at the heart of Total Defence.</p>
